INFORMATION AND ACTION NETWORK CENTRE (IAN CENTRE)

About IAN Centre

Information and Action Network Centre is a unique local Humanitarian and Development Network of individuals and institutions concerned with people exercising basic human rights, capable of influencing or determining the systems and structures which govern them. IAN CENTRE was founded and registered in Kenya in September 2007 to promote the sharing of knowledge, dialogue, and understanding of legal, political, and socio-economic systems necessary for contributing meaningfully to issues affecting development and making intelligent decisions about them.

The AIM is to infuse greater rights awareness amongst Kenyans because an aware person acts from an enlightened position.

Our Major Concerns

Our major concern is substantive democratic advancement and the rule of law. We ask whether Kenya will ever achieve substantive democratic advancement in the context of changes in political leadership and arrangements in a political context without principle. Any thinking Kenyan is able to worry about the future of this country.

Positioning for Change

  • Using technology to advance civic education on civilian oversight, citizen public debt audit, social audit, and public participation.
  • Stimulating debate about how people can shape the current and future decades in the country’s life.
  • Advocating for human rights defenders protection.
  • Increasing broad-based membership in networking to encourage support and foster a sense of community for social justice and rule of law.

Discussion Points

  • Democracy vs Culture of Impunity (corruption, economic crimes, public maladministration).
  • Complaints to oversight agencies.
  • Day-to-day struggles for political survival by elected leaders that inhibit addressing key agendas for democratic reforms.
  • Democracy and the missing dimensions.
  • Leadership that nurtures sustainable democratic political culture vs leadership that regresses the country into past politics of authoritarianism.
